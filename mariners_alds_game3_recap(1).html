ALDS · Game 3 · Rogers Centre · Oct. 14, 2030

Young Erupts With Homer, Two Doubles to Push Mariners to Brink of Sweep

Cole Young went 3-for-4 with a go-ahead two-run blast in the sixth inning as Seattle rallied from a 5-5 tie to beat Toronto 9–5 and take a commanding 3–0 series lead.

TORONTO — There was a moment in the bottom of the first, when Bryan Woo had given up back-to-back hits and Toronto led 2–0 inside a humming Rogers Centre, that it looked like the Blue Jays might finally have found something. They hadn't. Seattle's second baseman Cole Young had other ideas.

Young went 3-for-4 with a home run and two doubles, drove in three runs and scored once as the Mariners rallied past Toronto 9–5 to take a 3–0 series lead in the American League Division Series. Seattle needs just one more win to complete the sweep and punch their ticket to the ALCS.

6th Inning · The Go-Ahead Blast
With the score knotted at 5–5 and the series very much in the balance, Young stepped in against Austin Cates with a runner at first and deposited a 2-run home run into the seats. It was a series-turning moment — the Mariners never looked back, adding two more before the night was through.

Woo labored badly in his playoff start, exiting after just three innings having allowed five earned runs on seven hits. The bullpen, though, was immaculate. Matt Brash was handed the ball in the fourth with the score tied and delivered two scoreless innings to earn the win, while Luke Gilbert, Mason Montgomery, and Orion Kerkering combined to allow nothing over the final four frames.

Young had already set the tone before that pivotal sixth. He doubled twice off Cates — once in the second to drive in a pair and again in the fourth — before punctuating his evening with the long ball that put Seattle ahead for good. For the series, Young is now batting .400 with three RBI in three games.

5th Inning · Rodriguez Makes It 5–5
Julio Rodriguez launched a two-run homer off Cates in the fifth to draw Seattle even at 5–5 — his first of the postseason. Rodriguez finished 2-for-5 with three RBI, and Cal Raleigh added a solo shot in the fourth to keep the Mariners within striking distance.

Toronto starter Juan Soriano lasted just one inning, forcing Austin Cates into the game early. Cates was touched up for six runs and three home runs across 4⅓ innings, watching his series ERA balloon to 7.84. "Things aren't going great right now," Blue Jays manager John Schneider said after the game. "But you don't need me to tell you that."

Fernando Gonzalez added a solo homer in the ninth to cap the scoring. Seattle's Mariners, buoyed by a lineup that produced 12 hits and nine runs, now stand one game from ending this series in four.
